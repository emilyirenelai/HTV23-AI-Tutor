 Question: What is the main idea of this passage?
Answer: Connecting to databases can be expensive in terms of time, so it is efficient to connect once and have multiple database functions use the same connection.

Question: What makes the inefficient way of using a database connection inefficient?
Answer: Connecting and disconnecting multiple times every time the database is required.

Question: What is the singleton pattern? How is it used in database connections?
Answer: The singleton pattern involves setting up the database only once. Then, whichever methods require calling the database will leverage this one connection.

Question: Why is the type of design efficient in multiple ways?
Answer: It's fast and saves energy and processing power.

Question: What is the primary business concern with the type of design?
Answer: It is fast.
